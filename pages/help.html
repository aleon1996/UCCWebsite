What to look for

Demonstrating empathy and willingness to support a friend or student is an important part of budiling our strong community. Around campus in residence halls, classrooms and groups, factulty, staff and students may witness the early signs of distress in others. Students often seek initial assistance from other students, faculty and staff because they perceive the mto be available and willing to listen.

Individuals dealing with personsal issues of problems tend to show signs that they are strugglign in some way. Many students may experience changes in mood or anxiety. However, when symptoms of distress are persistence over a long period of time or when they interfere with academic responsibitlities and social relationships, it may be a cause for concern.
