What is group counseling and can it help me?

Each semester, the UCC offers a variety of group therapy experiences. In group therapy, can count on the expertise of the counselors who facilitate the group, and you can also get to know other students who are experiencing similar issues as you. Groups at the UCC are designed for students who want to understand themselves and others in greater depth. These may be students who have concerns about relationships with others; students who feel different from their peers because of the type of problem currently being experienced; and/or students who make choices based on what others want or expect instead of evaluating decisions for themselves. A common theme for group participants is wanting to learn how to change thinking, feelings, and/or behavior to feel better about themselves and cope more effectively with life.

Psychiatric Services

The UCC provides outpatient psychiatric services to eligible Notre Dame students who are receiving counseling at the UCC. Referrals for a psychiatric evaluation and treatment may be made following the initial assessment and are made as a joint decision between UCC staff and the client.

What is an initial consult and how can I schedule one?

An initial consult is the first step to getting the mental health treatment you need. In an initial consult, you will come into the UCC be given a consultation on your reasons for seeking help and your goals in doing so. You will fill out a form describing your reasons for seeking treatment and afterwards you will meet with a UCC therapist. After your leave your initial consult, you will be matched with a counselor and will begin your treatment at the UCC. You can schedule it by either calling the number below or visiting our schedule tab.
