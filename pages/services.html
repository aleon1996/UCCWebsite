What is individual counseling and can it help me?

In individual counseling, you have the full attention of one of our counselors in an one-on-one atmosphere of understanding, warmth, and insight. Our counselors can help you in a variety of ways because they are excellent sounding-boards, compassionate listeners, and skillful experts in problems of living.

Group Counseling

You've come to the right place!

What is group counseling and can it help me?

Each semester, the UCC offers a variety of group therapy experiences. In group therapy, can count on the expertise of the counselors who facilitate the group, and you can also get to know other students who are experiencing similar issues as you. Groups at the UCC are designed for students who want to understand themselves and others in greater depth. These may be students who have concerns about relationships with others; students who feel different from their peers because of the type of problem currently being experienced; and/or students who make choices based on what others want or expect instead of evaluating decisions for themselves. A common theme for group participants is wanting to learn how to change thinking, feelings, and/or behavior to feel better about themselves and cope more effectively with life.

Psychiatric Services

The UCC provides outpatient psychiatric services to eligible Notre Dame students who are receiving counseling at the UCC. Referrals for a psychiatric evaluation and treatment may be made following the initial assessment and are made as a joint decision between UCC staff and the client.

For students with psychiatric disabilities, click here to learn more about how to balance this and college.

If you think you psychiatric services are right for you, schedule an initial consult at the UCC.

Walk In Crisis Services

The UCC provides crisis services to all Notre Dame students. If you are in crisis and need to be seen that day, you can use our walk-in hours, 9:00am – 4:00pm, Monday through Friday, and be seen by an on-call crisis counselor. If possible, please call ahead (574-631-7336) and let us know that you are coming. Please tell the receptionist that you are in crisis and need to see someone as soon as possible.

  • Hours: Monday through Friday from 9:00am – 5:00pm*
  • Telephone: 574-631-7336
  • Fax: 574-631-5643

After hour help-line and urgent crisis services

After normal business hours, including weekend and holidays, telephone crisis assessment, consultation, and support is available by contacting us at 574-631-7336. Our after-hours counseling service is staffed by licensed counselors who can provide immediate assistance by phone regarding a variety of issues that require on-the-spot help or consultation, and can facilitate transition to UCC daytime services. Members of the Notre Dame community who are urgently concerned about a Notre Dame student, may also call for consultation.

Please note: UCC crisis services are not available when the University is closed during Christmas break. If the student of concern is outside of the local Notre Dame area, using local or national crisis resources may be a more appropriate option.

  • Hours: Monday through Friday from 9:00am – 5:00pm*
  • Telephone: 574-631-7336
  • Fax: 574-631-5643

Suicide Prevention and Coping Resources

Just about everyone has considered suicide, however fleetingly, at one time or another. There is no danger of “giving someone the idea.” In fact, it can be a great relief if you bring the questions of suicide into the open, and discuss it freely without showing shock or disapproval. Raising the question of suicide shows that you are taking the person seriously and responding to the potential of her or his distress.

    Initial consult

    You've come to the right place!

    What is an initial consult and how can I schedule one?

    An initial consult is the first step to getting the mental health treatment you need. In an initial consult, you will come into the UCC be given a consultation on your reasons for seeking help and your goals in doing so. You will fill out a form describing your reasons for seeking treatment and afterwards you will meet with a UCC therapist. After your leave your initial consult, you will be matched with a counselor and will begin your treatment at the UCC. You can schedule it by either calling the number below or visiting our schedule tab.

    • Come to UCC Reception Desk, 3rd Floor, St. Liam Hall
    • Telephone: 574-631-7336
